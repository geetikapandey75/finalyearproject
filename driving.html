Driving Licence in India

A Driving Licence (DL) is an official document issued by the Regional Transport Office (RTO) in India that permits an individual to drive a motor vehicle on public roads. It's mandatory for every driver to hold a valid licence.

Types of Driving Licences

  • Learning Licence: Temporary and issued for learning purposes.
  • Permanent Driving Licence: For private vehicle owners after passing a driving test.
  • Commercial Licence: For professional drivers of commercial vehicles.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be at least 18 years old (for a private vehicle).
  • 16 years for gearless two-wheelers (with parental consent).
  • Must have a valid Learner’s Licence for at least 30 days.

Documents Required

  • Proof of age (Aadhar Card, PAN Card, etc.)
  • Proof of address (Utility bills, Voter ID, etc.)
  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Application Form (Form 1, Form 2)

Application Process

  1. Visit the official Parivahan portal.
  2. Fill in the online application for Learner’s Licence.
  3. Book a slot for the Learner’s test at the RTO.
  4. After passing, apply for a Permanent Licence after 30 days.
  5. Take a driving test at the RTO for final approval.
